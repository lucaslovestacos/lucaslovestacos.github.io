Water Hyacinth: Introduction
The Water Hyacinth (Eichhornia crassipes) is an invasive species in the United States.

The Earth is home to many different species. Each area, continent, or biome, though, has specialized species that could be harmful if introduced to a different location.

Plants who are introduced to an ecosystem where they are harmful are called "invasive." Invasive species can unbalance an ecosystem where they don't belong by creating typically unusual levels of competition, over-hunting other wildlife in the area for food, or even potentially wiping out entire species.
