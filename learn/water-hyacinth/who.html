Water Hyacinth: Effect

The Water Hyacinth grows quickly and aggressively.

Image Credit to the Center of Aquatic and Invasive Plants

The rapid growth of the plant can cause competition for space and competition for nutrients and sunlight with the plants and even animals that grow there natively.

The leaves of the plant can blanket the surface of the water. This can prevent the water from absorbing oxygen, which can degrade the water and lower its oxygen content. This can negatively affect the animals who live in the water by making the water unlivable for them.
